Overview

Helping leadership move through a complex membership system transition with clearer, more reliable data.

The National Association of Flight Instructors needed execution support during a major transition from Tovuti to Member365. The organization had 50+ spreadsheets and data exports covering 16,500+ members and their corresponding information, including user records, subscription records, transaction history, coursework, Stripe payment information, and membership templates. Some individual source spreadsheets exceeded 30,000 records.

AeroVista Analytics coordinated with the new system teams, cleaned and reconciled the source data, migrated the information into the new system, and performed quality assurance after migration so leadership could make confident decisions throughout the transition.

The Challenge

Several systems, many record types, and high-stakes classification decisions.

NAFI's membership data included active members, lapsed members, lifetime members, organization members, auto-renewing subscriptions, transaction history, course records, and contact tags. Because the data came from several systems, inconsistencies appeared across records, including duplicate emails, missing renewal dates, conflicting membership statuses, incomplete payment histories, and members who appeared in one system but not another.

During the data preparation and audit process, AeroVista identified that the previous system had labeled some members as active even though subscription end dates and transaction history showed they were inactive or lapsed. That issue had to be reconciled before the migrated data could support an accurate count of true membership.

The organization needed a reliable way to determine which records should be imported, how members should be classified, and where additional review was required before launch.

AeroVista's Role

Migration coordination, data migration, reconciliation, reporting, and post-migration quality assurance.

Consolidated, reviewed, and migrated data from 50+ spreadsheets and supporting exports. Coordinated with the new system teams during the Tovuti to Member365 migration process. Worked with data covering 16,500+ members and corresponding transaction, coursework, subscription, payment, and membership information. Handled individual source spreadsheets with more than 30,000 records. Cleaned and prepared membership import templates for Member365. Reconciled user, subscription, transaction, course, and payment data. Discovered active member mislabels by reconciling subscription end dates with transaction history. Identified duplicate, missing, mismatched, and incomplete member records. Validated active versus lapsed member classification. Performed quality assurance after migration to review migrated records and identify issues requiring follow-up. Created organized spreadsheets and reports for leadership review. Investigated individual member records when payment or membership status needed verification. Communicated findings clearly for executive and Board-level decision-making.

Outcome

Thousands of member records migrated and reviewed through post-migration QA.

AeroVista Analytics migrated NAFI's data into Member365 and validated the results after migration. The project corrected visibility into true active membership, renewal information, duplicate records, lapsed members, and data gaps that needed attention before and after launch.

After the migration and QA process, leadership had migrated member data in the new system and a more accurate count of true membership based on reconciled subscription and transaction evidence.

Business Impact

Turning scattered operational data into decision-ready information.

Through this project, AeroVista Analytics helped turn scattered operational data into structured, reviewable, and decision-ready information. The work advanced a critical system migration while also creating a stronger foundation for future membership reporting, renewal tracking, and revenue analysis.

For membership-based organizations, stronger data visibility means clearer insight into active members, lapsed members, renewals, payment history, and data quality issues that can directly affect operations, revenue, and leadership decisions.
